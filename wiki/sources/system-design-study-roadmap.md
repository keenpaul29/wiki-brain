# Curated System Design Study Roadmap

## Summary

Warns against passive video consumption for system design interview prep, laying out a structured learning curriculum of books, blogs, courses, and engineering resources to build true trade-off intuition.

## Key Ideas

- Passive consumption of system design videos leads to poor retention; system design requires active mental model construction and tradeoff evaluation under pressure.
- Interviewers assess the capability to reason through trade-offs, identify points of failure at scale, ask clarifying questions, and choose simpler designs when complexity is unwarranted.
- Canonical resources are structured to guide learning: System Design Primer (foundations), Alex Xu (applied concepts), designgurus.io (pattern recognition), and ByteByteGo (visuals).
- Real-world engineering depth lies in reading company engineering blogs (e.g., Netflix, Uber, AWS, Google, Meta) and High Scalability case studies to see real failure post-mortems and trade-offs.
- LeetCode System Design discussions help candidates calibrate their responses based on feedback from actual tech interviews.
- Mastery requires live mock practice, recording oneself, and verbalizing requirements extraction under pressure rather than just accumulating technical vocabulary.
