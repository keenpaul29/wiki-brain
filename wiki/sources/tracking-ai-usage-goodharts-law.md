# Goodhart's Law in Corporate AI Usage Tracking

## Summary

Explores how big tech companies' attempts to mandate and track developer AI token consumption resulted in 'tokenmaxxing' (gaming metrics to inflate usage numbers), highlighting the consequences of Goodhart's Law.

## Key Ideas

- Goodhart's Law states that when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure.
- Tech companies (Amazon, Meta, Microsoft) implemented dashboards to track employee AI token usage to prove AI productivity gains and justify massive Capex budgets.
- Amazon mandated that 80%+ of developers use AI tools weekly, leading to employee skepticism and fear that these metrics would affect performance reviews.
- Employees hacked these metrics via tokenmaxxing—running background agents (e.g. MeshClaw) to artificially inflate token consumption through fake deployments, emails, and Slack traffic.
- Perverse incentives led to inflated demand signals, casting doubt on the metrics companies present to investors to justify massive AI Capex (projected at $700-725B in 2026).
- NVIDIA's CEO framed token consumption as a performance benchmark ($250k of tokens annually per $500k engineer), fueling top-down pressure.
- The industry is beginning to pivot from tracking raw token volume to measuring efficient token usage to combat metrics gaming.
