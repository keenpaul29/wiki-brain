# AI Developer Cognitive Archetypes

## Summary

This source reflects on the cognitive cost of AI-assisted development. It distinguishes supportive, mixed, and risky AI modes, then maps repeated habits into archetypes ranging from AI Architect to AI Passenger.

## Key Ideas

- Supportive AI modes include explanation, tradeoff exploration, critique, assumption testing, and concept clarification.
- Mixed modes such as boilerplate, refactoring suggestions, and documentation can save time while compressing understanding if used carelessly.
- Risky modes include blindly accepting generated solutions, delegating architecture too early, and handing off debugging without understanding root cause.
- Reflective prompts before, during, and after work help preserve ownership: attempt first, test assumptions, explain the result, and distinguish leverage from dependency.
- The archetypes are awareness tools: AI Architect, AI Balancer, Autopilot Builder, and AI Passenger.
